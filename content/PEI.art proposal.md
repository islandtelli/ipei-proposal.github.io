Growing the artists, organizations, and businesses of PEI’s creative sector through the development and promotion of a shared online directory.
### Overview

Currently pei.art exists as a compelling online directory highlighting PEI’s creative sector professionals, businesses, and organizations. 

Each profile on the platform includes all of the information a member needs to promote their creative practice, including biographies, artist statements, photo galleries, embedded audio and video files, business hours, location map and directions, and a contact form. 

Its initial development was made possible with funding from the Canada Council for the Arts, and Innovation PEI. 

With limited promotion, over 250 members have created media-rich profiles and some have begun to receive business inquiries through the platform. However, without resources to invest in further improvement and marketing of the service, its growth has slowed, leaving much potential unrealized.

We propose that it can quickly progress into a powerful marketing platform that enables the cost-effective promotion of all members. 

We propose to extend this strong foundation into a sophisticated and scalable shared marketing and e-commerce platform that will become an essential destination for directory members and visitors alike.

### Phase 2 of 4 - Addition of e-Commerce functionality

- Elegant appointment booking functionality will allow members to manage bookings and receive payment for in-person or virtual performances, studio visits, or consultations. 
- The ability to sell gift cards will allow for “impulse” sales.
- One-click upsell functionality will help increase revenues without needing an increase in traffic.



### Phase 3 of 4 - Marketing

  The final phase will see us unlock the potential of the platform through promotion to **four key audiences**. 

1. **Potential members**, whose stories, visions, and works will attract and delight visitors will be targeted via direct outreach and local print, outdoor, radio, and online advertising. 
2. **PEI residents** who will be awakened to the amazing talent in their backyard will also be targeted via local print, outdoor, radio and online advertising. 
3. **Visitors to PEI** (over 1 million per year), who wish to maintain a connection to our island’s creators will be reached via rack cards with QR codes in tourist industry establishments and online ad retargeting. 
4. **Globally distributed art lovers** will be reached via competitive Search Engine Optimization and paid Search Engine Advertisements.

### Rationale

####  Why now?

**We wish to undertake these activities** because we recognize that:

- Our members improve our communities in innumerable ways and we consider investing in their practices as investing in an array of activities that contribute to our province.
- Artists, creators, businesses, and organizations lack the resources and training or desire to invest in marketing their practices. 
- Those competent in social media marketing can still have their investments derailed by the pivots of distant corporations. 


#### **Goals and Outcomes**

- Our primary **goal** is to support artistic practice by making PEI.art the top-of-mind destination for people who have some kind of connection to Prince Edward Island's creative sector and who would delight in strengthening that connection. 
- Our platform will greatly improve the artists' ability to fully capitalize upon this existing market pull.
- The most straightforward **outcomes** of this project will be an exponential increase in functionality, member participation, and audience visitation. 

#### **Needs or gaps being filled**

- Artists, creators, small businesses, and non-profit organizations need increased visibility but lack the time, skills, money, or other resources to invest in promoting their market-ready products and services. 
- Our project will address these **gaps** by creating a scalable marketing and sales platform for thousands of members with no cost to the stakeholders themselves. 
- By focusing marketing efforts on one URL and several keywords, we can bring far more attention to the creative sector’s stakeholders than they may ever be able to on their own, serving as a powerful and efficient multiplier. 
- Including an array of disciplines creates connections and builds a sense of belonging to a larger community. 
- Showcasing our sector's members in a modern manner demonstrates our community is export-ready. 
- The “You Might Also Like” discovery feature leverages searches for one creator into exposure for many others. 
- Scalable e-commerce that does not require managing physical inventory and leverages basic levels of automation creates new revenue opportunities for artists.  

#### Increasing support for the sector and innovation and collaboration within it.

How are these needs and gaps being addressed in a way that is either **innovative** or that fosters **increased sectoral support** and **collaboration**? 

Our project creates the opportunity to foster increased sectoral support from government and the private sector by presenting potential funders with a straightforward mechanism to invest broadly in the sector without needing to choose specific creators, organizations or projects to support. Opportunities for sectoral collaboration are increased through high-level involvement of PEI arts service organizations working in Dance, Literature, Media Arts, Music and Sound, Theatre, and Visual Arts.

By promoting their own profiles, members also raise awareness of the directory in general and send traffic to their peers via the “You Might Also Like” function. A search for a specific theatre can introduce the searcher to the entire community including other theatres, actors, playwrights, and backstage and administrative professionals.
  
#### Who will be the primary beneficiaries? 

The primary beneficiaries of our project will be members seeking an audience to perform for, sell to, or otherwise engage. Creators and artists seeking other artists to hire or collaborate with will also benefit. PEI residents will fill with pride and inspiration as they learn more about the amazing creators in their communities, while visitors will more easily maintain a connection to our culture. Art lovers anywhere will benefit from being able to discover a new favourite artist or artwork.

#### Strengthening and developing practices in the arts sector

1.11 How does your project propose to strengthen and develop practices in the arts sector? For projects proposing organizational development activities, how does your project propose to strengthen and develop the organization itself and how will this be shared with the arts sector? (approximately 250 words, 1700 characters)

  

Our project will strengthen and develop practices in the arts sector by giving artists, cultural workers and organizations higher visibility, new work opportunities, new collaboration or training options, and new revenue streams. It will also help reduce the stress and cognitive burden of needing to constantly learn new strategies to advance the marketing aspects of their careers. Hopefully our project will lead to increased incomes which will, in turn, lead to members having more freedom, time, money,

and other resources to invest in developing their practices.

  

While not our main focus, this project will also help strengthen and develop CreativePEI by giving us the opportunity to do a great deal of outreach to the sector and to develop stronger relationships with artists, cultural workers and organizations. We’ll also strengthen our business administration skills by developing new information and content management workflows, and our financial management capacity. We’ll grow our relationship with independent contractors in the fields of Wordpress website development, Search Engine Optimization and Inbound Marketing. This will be shared with the arts sector in our day-to-day operations. As we continue to develop the skills and knowledge required to make our project a success, we’ll be able to share this expertise with those creative sector workers who are looking to take their career journey even further.

#### Inclusion

1.12 How does your project propose to benefit a broad and inclusive representation of your artistic community, particularly with regards to the engagement and inclusion of Indigenous communities, culturally diverse groups, people who are Deaf or have disabilities, and official language minority communities? (approximately 250 words, 1700 characters).


Our platform will be at its most compelling and most effective when its membership is as broad and inclusive a representation of our community as is possible. Ultimately we want every single member of Prince Edward Island’s creative community included on our platform. Every member will benefit from every other member’s participation by increasing the amount of traffic that is drawn to the site, and by helping to provide a more compelling experience once the visitor has arrived. One of the greatest hopes we have for our project is that it is able to showcase the entire spectrum of creators on Prince Edward Island

and better illuminate those who may be traditionally underrepresented, including Indigenous artists, culturally diverse creators, culture workers with disabilities, and those from official language minority communities. Creators who may have difficulty getting media exposure, promoting themselves on social media, getting exhibited, or otherwise participating in the community or the marketplace will find a home in our directory and will be presented equally alongside their peers.

Since 2020 he has overseen over $300,000 worth of online training projects for CreativePEI and has lead a brand refresh, rebuilt the organization's website, relaunched its social media strategy and monthly newsletter and overseen the development and promotion of the PEI.art directory. At Celtic Colours he grew the festival’s social media audience from 0 to over 20,000, oversaw production of promotional videos that accumulated more than 100,000 views collectively, and grew website traffic annually for five years. He helped launch major sponsorship and live video streaming initiatives. During this period the Festival was honoured with two national tourism industry awards, broke ticket sales records in 2012 and 2013, and entered into the largest sponsor partnership in its 20 year history.
